Andee Wallace

Andee Wallace is the co-founder of Robigo, a company using synthetic biology to create eco-friendly crop treatments. Robigo develops a natural, bacteria-based pesticides found in soil and plants, which are bioengineered to fight harmful diseases without the need for toxic chemicals. Andee and Robigo's approach helps make farming more sustainable and reduces the reliance on harmful pesticides. Wallace's work represents intersectionality through her contributions in both environmental sustainablity and gender diveristy. She makes huge strides in her field for being a black female scientist.

Marc Hannah

Marc Hannah is an electrical engineer and computer graphics designer who co-founded Silicon Graphics, Inc with 6 others. Silicon Graphics, Inc became very well knwon for its computer graphics technology in many well-known productions. Hannah was responsible for the creation of computer programs that were used to create effects for movies such as Jurassic Park, Aladdin, Beauty and the Beast, The Hunt for Red October, Field of Dreams, television programs, and the opening introduction of Monday Night Football. Marc Hannah has one several awards, including the Professional Achievment Award from the Illinois Institute of Technology and the National Technical Association. When watching movies and productions with graphics, it is easy to forget that someone is behind the screen creating computer programs doing all the work. Hannah paved the way for graphic effects in movie and television production. As a black male in a predominantly white tech field, Hannah's career shows the importance of diversity in innovative work spaces.
